## Key Findings by Thread

### T1: Clinical & Cognitive Science

**Prevalence:**
- CDC/MMWR (2024): 15.5 million U.S. adults have ADHD diagnosis (Oct-Nov 2023 data). URL: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/wr/mm7340a1.htm
- Global estimate: 4–5% of adults (NIMH). Source: arxiv.org/html/2507.06864

**DSM-5-TR executive function model:**
- Barkley's EF deficit theory: ADHD involves reduced executive control associated with dopamine-signaling deficits in PFC, basal ganglia, thalamus, amygdala circuits.
- Source: Frontiers | Arousal dysregulation and executive dysfunction in ADHD (2023). https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2023.1336040/full

**Dopamine & task initiation:**
- ADHD involves deficits in initiatory actions specifically (not just follow-through) — a 2023 Nature paper found impaired value-estimation for first-stage/initiatory actions in ADHD. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41398-023-02717-7.pdf (PDF, metadata only)
- Stimulant medication modulates dopamine and norepinephrine, enhancing task-related networks and suppressing DMN. Source: Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews (2024). https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0149763424003105

**Time blindness:**
- Time perception impairment is well-established in adult ADHD across multiple domains (estimation, reproduction, production, discrimination). Review of past 10 years of studies: IJERPH (2023). https://www.mdpi.com/1660-4601/20/4/3098
- "Internal time runs faster for children with ADHD" (Barkley et al., 1997; Rubia et al., 1997 — scalar expectancy theory). Multiple replications.
- Reviewed in PMC: Clinical Implications of Time Perception in ADHD. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6556068/

**Working memory & digital environments:**
- Neurodivergent students have significantly higher perceived extraneous cognitive load in online learning. N=231 survey. MDPI Education Sciences (2024). https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7102/14/5/516
- Tab overload as a WM problem: adults with ADHD have multiple tabs as externalized WM; tab switching frequency correlates with reduced attention. Source: Focus Bear/RMIT proof-of-concept (Nagel et al., 2025, R²=0.77). https://nafath.mada.org.qa/nafath-article/mcn2903/
- ADHD survey (n=25 professionals): 37% report 21+ browser tabs open at any given time; 80%+ bookmark content they never return to. Source: arXiv 2507.06864 (2025).

**Hyperfocus:**
- Hyperfocus is real but poorly defined. Springer Current Psychology (2023) study examined relations between hyperfocus, ADHD, and affective dysfunction. https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12144-023-05235-3
- Hyperfocus + internet: Frontiers Psychiatry (2023) — hyperfocus mediates internet addiction in ADHD. Higher trait hyperfocus predicts IA. https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2023.1127777/full
- PLOS ONE (2023) N=1220 adults: ADHD distractibility includes external distraction, intrusive thoughts, AND mind-wandering — three distinct factors. Hyperfocus is a separate phenomenon explaining ~80% of variance in distractibility factors. https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0292215

**Rejection Sensitivity Dysphoria (RSD):**
- RSD is emotional pain triggered by perceived rejection/criticism. Not a formal DSM diagnosis but well-supported phenomenologically.
- PLOS ONE 2024 qualitative study: "lived experience of rejection sensitivity in ADHD." https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0314669
- Cleveland Clinic clinical summary: RSD linked to ADHD via brain structure differences in emotion regulation. https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/24099-rejection-sensitive-dysphoria-rsd
- ADDitude Magazine: RSD as manifestation of emotional dysregulation in adult ADHD — not addressed by CBT or DBT alone. https://www.additudemag.com/rejection-sensitive-dysphoria-adhd-emotional-dysregulation/

**Internet addiction & ADHD comorbidity:**
- Meta-analysis (J Psychiatric Research, 2023): PIU significantly correlated with inattention (r moderate), hyperactivity/impulsivity in N=18,859. https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0022395623004703
- Inhibitory control moderates inattention→internet addiction pathway. PMC 2024. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12135584/
- Genes and screens: BJPsych review — heavy media multitasking harms working memory and task-switching for ADHD brains (citing Ophir, Nass & Wagner "heavy media multitaskers" landmark study). https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/the-british-journal-of-psychiatry/article/genes-and-screens-attentiondeficit-hyperactivity-disorder-in-the-digital-age/D692E6A269DD6FCB2CF965814270EEB9

**Digital interventions effectiveness (meta-level):**
- Systematic review of 26 systematic reviews (BMC Psychiatry, 2025; N=34,442): Digital interventions show positive effects on ADHD symptoms but "evidence generally low quality." https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12888-025-06825-0
- Meta-analysis of 25 RCTs (J Affective Disorders, 2024; N=1780): digital interventions significant reduction in ADHD symptoms (SMD=−0.33). https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0165032724013910

### T2: HCI/UX Research

**WCAG / COGA Guidelines:**
- W3C COGA (Cognitive Accessibility at W3C): "Making Content Usable for People with Cognitive and Learning Disabilities" — Design Guide with 8 Objectives. Covers ADHD explicitly. Key patterns: predictable navigation, low visual clutter, minimal cognitive load, error-tolerant design. https://www.w3.org/TR/coga-usable/design_guide.html
- WCAG 2.2 covers learning disabilities but is acknowledged as insufficient for ADHD; COGA provides supplemental guidance. https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/Overview.html
- Section508.gov (2024): 13.9% of U.S. adults have cognitive disability; ADHD included. Design principles include minimizing distractions. https://www.section508.gov/design/digital-content-users-with-cognitive-disabilities/

**HCI papers (ADHD + browser/digital UX):**
- CHI 2026: Reading with Diversity in Mind — eye-tracking/pupillometry study with and without ADHD readers. Font and font size have significant effects; ADHD status has "large effect size" on reading speed. https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772363.3799383
- INTERACT 2025: "Towards Inclusive Guidelines for Web Design for Adults with ADHD" — preliminary comparative study. https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-032-05008-3_59
- IUI 2024: "Evaluating ADHD Users' Experience with Recommender Systems" — ADHD users have distinct experience with recommendation systems. https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3640544.3645222
- CHI 2023 (CUI workshop): Conversational UIs for adults with ADHD — co-design study. https://cui.acm.org/workshops/CHI2023/pdfs/nordberg_Inclusive_Conversational_User_Interfaces_for_Adults_with_ADHD_Final.pdf (PDF metadata only)
- CHI 2023: "When Browsing Gets Cluttered: Browsing Clutter, Browsing Habits, and Coping" — tab overload studied. Half of participants perceive tab overload as a problem. https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3544548.3580690

**Browser Reader Mode:**
- CHI 2019 (Microsoft Research): Firefox Reader View significantly reduces visual complexity; improves reading speed. https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/the-impact-of-web-browser-reader-views-on-reading-speed-and-user-experience/
- BOIA accessibility blog: Reader mode specifically benefits users with ADHD and dyslexia by removing ads, formatting clutter. https://www.boia.org/blog/how-browser-reader-modes-improve-the-experiences-of-people-with-disabilities

**Distraction blocking (in-situ study):**
- Mark et al., UbiComp 2017 (field study, N=32 info workers, 5+5 days): Website blockers significantly increased focus and productivity vs. baseline. "How blocking distractions affects workplace focus and productivity." https://www.interruptions.net/literature/Mark-UbiComp17.pdf (PDF metadata only)
- RCT: Blocking mobile internet (1-month RCT): improved sustained attention, mental health, and subjective well-being. https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/322a/0fc8c72f6dc5672ab587904a7efa888be3b7.pdf (PDF metadata only)

**Purpose Mode study:**
- "Purpose Mode" browser extension: toggling off attention-capturing design patterns (ACDPs). Participants spent 21 fewer daily minutes on social media; distraction reduced in 7% of EMA responses (vs. baseline). hanklee.com paper. https://hankhplee.com/papers/purpose_mode.pdf (PDF metadata only)

**Cognitive accessibility + psychophysiology:**
- PLOS ONE (2025): Cognitive accessibility features (simplified language, no distracting context) enhance cognitive engagement even in users without disabilities. Eye-tracking evidence. https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0328552

**Tab overload and task-centric browsing:**
- Tabs.do (UIST 2021): Task-centric Chrome extension groups tabs by project, reducing tab overload. https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3472749.3474777
- Focus Bear proof-of-concept (2025, n=6 RMIT): ML on tab-switching, idle time, task-relevance achieves R²=0.77 predicting attention. https://nafath.mada.org.qa/nafath-article/mcn2903/

### T3: Tooling Landscape

**FocusUp (KIT, 2025) — most ADHD-specific browser extension:**
- Published at W4A 2025 (Web for All conference): Chrome extension for ADHD; features: task list tied to websites, reading progress bar. Designed at KIT h-lab.
- "Findings suggest FocusUp offers a promising and effective approach to enhancing focus, task execution, and web-based productivity." (self-reported user study)
- Authors: Aljedaani, Leonard, Ruiz, Wegener. https://publikationen.bibliothek.kit.edu/1000185883/168296537

**Commercial distraction blockers:**
- Freedom: Blocks sites/apps cross-device. WEIS 2017 study: two treatment conditions vs. baseline; "Freedom" measurably improved productivity. https://weis2021.econinfosec.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2017/06/WEIS_2017_paper_26.pdf (PDF metadata only)
- StayFocusd, LeechBlock: Time-based blockers. No ADHD-specific RCTs found.
- Forest: Virtual tree growth tied to focus sessions. UBC 2026 study (CHI doi:10.1145/3772318.3790801) found Forest causes shame in neurodivergent users: "amount of time focused became a shameful point of comparison." N=27 neurodivergent students.
- Cold Turkey Blocker, Apple Screen Time: Rigid blockers. Same UBC study noted inflexible designs clash with ADHD hyperfocus and time blindness.

**Built-in browser features:**
- Firefox Reader View: Removes ads, sidebars, popups; customizable font/size/spacing/theme. https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/firefox-reader-view-clutter-free-web-pages
- No published ADHD-specific RCT for any built-in browser focus mode found.
- Horse Browser: Replaces tabs with "Trails" (branching visual history). Designed for ADHD (externalizing executive function). https://browser.horse/adhd
- Neurodivergent Browsing Assistant (Misha Labs): "Compassionate Chrome extension for ADHD, autism, neurodivergent minds. Zero shame and zero tracking." Features: reader mode, tab management, calm browsing. https://browse.mishalabs.com/
- LumiRead (Northeastern, 2025): Chrome extension — disables pop-up ads, dyslexia-friendly fonts, TTS. https://techxplore.com/news/2025-07-chrome-extension-web-accessible-neurodivergent.html

**AI-integrated productivity for ADHD:**
- Nami AI: Neurodivergent-first AI assistant. External memory, energy tracking, adaptive interface. https://namiai.app/
- EveryMango: Gentle project management. No red badges, no overdue shame. https://everymango.com/
- MindFrame iO: ADHD task management, free. https://www.mindframe-io.com/

**Body doubling (digital):**
- ACM TACCESS 2024: Survey N=220 neurodivergent people — body doubling uses social presence to start/stay on task. Can be collocated or remote. https://dl.acm.org/doi/full/10.1145/3689648
- FLOWN/Neurodiversity in Business self-report survey (N=117 adults with ADHD): Virtual body doubling → over 2x increase in sustained focus; faster task re-engagement. https://neurodiversity.org/virtual-body-doubling-for-adhd-new-research-findings-from-117-adults/ (industry survey, confidence: medium)
- NPR (2024): Body doubling goes mainstream; experts describe it as "really effective." https://www.npr.org/2024/07/25/nx-s1-4860330/come-study-with-me-how-virtual-buddy-might-help-you-get-things-done

**Neurodivergent-aware productivity framework (arxiv 2025):**
- arXiv 2507.06864: AI+HITL framework; behavioral sensing (tab churn, idle time, app switching); on-device ML; "body doubling mode"; privacy-first. Co-designed with 25 ADHD professionals. Key findings:
  - 37% have 21+ browser tabs open at any time
  - 80%+ bookmark but never return to content
  - 70%+ frequently pulled away from high-priority tasks
  - Users preferred gentle pop-up reminders (55%), scheduled quiet check-ins (54%), weekly usage summaries (59%)
  - 77% said privacy is "very important" or "mandatory"
  - Tool fatigue + shame from neurotypical productivity apps widely reported

### T4: Shame vs. Support Design

**UBC CHI 2026 study (primary source):**
- Chow, Hariadi, McGrenere (UBC). Study of 27 neurodivergent post-secondary students using distraction blockers (Forest, Freedom, Apple Screen Time).
- CHI paper: doi:10.1145/3772318.3790801. Source: https://news.ubc.ca/2026/06/focus-apps-are-failing-neurodivergent-minds-new-research-finds/
- Key findings:
  - Inflexible timer-based designs clash with EF variability and hyperfocus
  - "Digital stimming" — intentionally using distractions to self-regulate and ease task entry — was a vital strategy blocked by current tools
  - Users felt shame about focused duration; worried about becoming dependent ("a crutch")
  - Three design recommendations: (1) support curated digital stimming, (2) task-based rules over timers, (3) scaffolds framed with affirming language not shame
- Confidence: HIGH (peer-reviewed CHI 2026)

**Mad In America (2026):**
- "The Compliance Machine: How ADHD Habit-Tracking Apps Enforce the Disease Model" — critique of streaks, avatar death, accountability surveillance features. https://www.madinamerica.com/2026/03/the-compliance-machine-how-adhd-habit-tracking-apps-enforce-the-disease-model/
- Confidence: MEDIUM (commentary, not peer-reviewed; matches peer-reviewed UBC findings)

**Gamification trilemma:**
- MDPI Information (2025): "Gamification in Digital Mental Health Interventions: A Systematic Review of the Engagement–Efficacy–Ethics Trilemma." Uncritical gamification harms ethics and efficacy; engagement ≠ clinical outcomes. https://www.mdpi.com/2078-2498/17/2/168 [Note: URL in search result was slightly different, used 2078-2489/17/2/168]

**Self-determination theory:**
- SDT (Deci & Ryan): autonomy, competence, relatedness as psychological needs. ADHD research shows need frustration may underlie psychopathology.
- JMIR Formative 2025: ADAPT framework RCT feasibility (Champ et al., n=23). SDT-based intervention improved psychological distress (p<.05), ADHD symptoms (inattention p<.01), QoL outcomes — without targeting symptoms directly. Key insight: "deficit-based etiology of ADHD may inadvertently contribute to social stigma and neurotype dysphoria." https://formative.jmir.org/2025/1/e69943
- SAGE 2021: "Studying Motivation in ADHD: The Role of Internal Motives and the Relevance of SDT." https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10870547211050948

**Neuroaffirmative design:**
- Horse Browser: "Your brain isn't broken. Your browser is." — explicit rejection of restriction-as-design, shame-as-motivation. https://browser.horse/adhd
- Horse Browser overstimulation page: "The ads are still there. The popups are still firing. Restricting yourself doesn't reduce the sensory noise. It just limits what you're allowed to see." https://browser.horse/adhd/overstimulation
- EveryMango, MindFrame iO, Nami AI — market explicitly rejects shame framing; built on support/scaffold framing.

**Work performance:**
- Cambridge Core (2025): "Work Performance Challenges and Needs of Adults with ADHD" — qualitative study. Underexplored subjective experience of workplace challenges. https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/european-psychiatry/article/work-performance-challenges-and-needs-of-adults-with-adhd-exploring-lived-experiences/F89D938734C530D9ED3EA51C9E104DA0
- arXiv 2507.06864: Survey participants described "tool fatigue and productivity shame" from neurotypical productivity apps; wanted "non-judgmental, presence-based support rather than gamified motivation or hard deadlines."
