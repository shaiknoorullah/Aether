# Researcher Brief T1 — Tab Overload & Context-Switching Cost

## Your job
Search for and collect quantitative evidence on:

1. **Tab overload**: How many tabs do knowledge workers keep open on average? Any surveys with sample sizes. Look for data from browser vendors (Google, Mozilla, Microsoft), UX research firms, productivity blogs with cited surveys.

2. **Context-switching cost in the browser**: Time cost to switch tabs/windows, time to recover focus/context, multitasking penalties. Academic sources: Gloria Mark (UC Irvine), Iqbal & Bailey (CHI papers), Microsoft Research productivity studies. Industry: RescueTime data, Atlassian/Slack productivity reports.

3. **Cognitive load from tab proliferation**: Working memory overload, navigation time, "tab amnesia" (opening a tab and forgetting why). Any eye-tracking or task-completion studies.

4. **Quantitative productivity costs**: Minutes per day lost to context switching, percentage productivity loss attributed to browser fragmentation.

## Search angles
- "average number of browser tabs open knowledge workers" survey
- "context switching cost productivity minutes" study
- "tab overload cognitive load browser" research
- "Gloria Mark interruption recovery time" study
- "browser multitasking productivity loss" quantitative
- "RescueTime context switching browser"
- Microsoft Research "task switching browser"
