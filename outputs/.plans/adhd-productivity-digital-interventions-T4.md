# Researcher Brief T4: Digital Interventions for Adult ADHD EF — Apps, Digital Therapeutics, AI Tools (2020–2026)

## Your job
Search for peer-reviewed and high-quality evidence on **digital interventions** targeting executive function and self-regulation in adults with ADHD: mobile apps, gamified cognitive training, digital therapeutics (DTx), reminders/external scaffolding tools, wearables, AI-assisted tools, and telehealth. Focus 2020–2026.

## Key questions to answer
1. What RCTs or feasibility studies exist for mobile apps targeting ADHD EF in adults (e.g., task management, reminder apps, cognitive training apps)? Effect sizes?
2. Are there FDA-cleared or CE-marked digital therapeutics for adult ADHD? What is their evidence base?
3. What is the evidence for gamified cognitive training (e.g., working memory training, inhibition training) delivered digitally in adults with ADHD?
4. Do external scaffolding tools (calendars, reminders, structured planning apps) show measurable EF improvement vs control in adults with ADHD?
5. Any evidence for AI-assisted tools (chatbots, conversational agents, LLM-based coaches) for ADHD self-regulation?
6. What are adherence/engagement rates in digital ADHD interventions? What predicts dropout?
7. What are the major gaps in digital ADHD intervention research (lack of active controls, short follow-up, publication bias)?

## Search strategy
Use paper search AND web search. Try queries:
- "digital intervention adult ADHD app RCT 2020 2021 2022 2023 2024"
- "mobile app ADHD executive function"
- "digital therapeutic ADHD adults"
- "gamified cognitive training ADHD adults"
- "ADHD reminder app scaffolding self-regulation"
- "AI chatbot ADHD coaching"
- "wearable ADHD attention monitoring"
- "EndeavorRx ADHD adult" (FDA-cleared DTx, check adult evidence)
- "ADHD digital health systematic review"

## Output format
Write a structured research note to: `outputs/.drafts/adhd-productivity-digital-interventions-research-T4.md`

Include:
- For each study: intervention name/type, sample (n, adult vs child), design, outcome measures, effect sizes, adherence data, authors, year, journal, URL/DOI
- Confidence level (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW)
- Whether the intervention is FDA/CE cleared
- Key caveats
- All search queries used
- Mark URLs UNVERIFIED if not confirmed

## Critical rules
- No PDF fetching
- No invented studies, app names, or statistics
- Record NOT FOUND for unavailable sources
- Distinguish adult from pediatric evidence explicitly
