# T3 Brief: Maintenance Sustainability & Monetization

## Focus
The engineering cost of maintaining a browser fork and viable monetization models that don't rely on surveillance.

## Key Questions
1. What does it cost to maintain a Chromium fork? How fast does upstream Chromium move? How many engineers does Brave/Vivaldi/Edge dedicate? Any public statements on patch burden?
2. What about Firefox forks? (Waterfox, Librewolf, Zen Browser, Mullvad Browser) — maintenance burden, volunteer vs. paid?
3. Mozilla's financial situation — revenue sources, dependency on Google search deal, sustainability concerns.
4. Brave's monetization: BAT token economics, Brave Search, Brave VPN, Leo AI. Revenue figures if public. User count trajectory.
5. Vivaldi's business model — how do they make money? Search deals? Sustainability?
6. Subscription browser models — did any work? (Mighty was $30/month, SigmaOS had pricing, Sidekick pricing)
7. What other monetization options exist? Enterprise licensing, white-label, marketplace/extensions store fees, premium features.
8. Open source browser sustainability — funding models (donations, grants, corporate sponsors).

## Instructions
- Use web search. Search for: "Chromium fork maintenance cost", "Brave revenue 2025", "Brave user count", "Mozilla Google search deal", "Vivaldi business model", "browser monetization without ads", "Brave BAT economics".
- Look for Brave's transparency reports or annual reports.
- Look for Mozilla Foundation/Corporation financial reports.
- Capture specific dollar amounts, engineer counts, user numbers with sources.
- Write findings to `outputs/.drafts/browser-failure-redteam-research-sustainability.md`.
- Do NOT fetch PDF URLs.

## Output Format
Markdown with financial data, engineer estimates, and business model analysis. All numbers sourced.
