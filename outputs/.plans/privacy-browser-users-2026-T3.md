# Researcher Brief T3: Telemetry Audits + Local-First / No-Cloud Expectations

**Output file:** `outputs/.drafts/privacy-browser-users-2026-research-T3.md`

## Your mission
Research what telemetry privacy-focused browser users refuse, how they audit browsers for telemetry, and what local-first/no-cloud demands they have for sync, history, and data storage.

## Questions to answer

### Telemetry Refusal

1. What telemetry does **Firefox** collect by default in 2025-2026? What does enabling/disabling it involve?
   - Mozilla Telemetry, Crash Reporter, Studies, Normandy
   - Recent controversies (Privacy-Preserving Attribution API, terms of service changes 2025)

2. What telemetry does **Chrome/Chromium** collect?

3. What telemetry does **Brave** collect? (Brave Rewards, P3A — Private Product Analytics)

4. What does **LibreWolf** disable vs. Firefox defaults?

5. What does **Mullvad Browser** disable?

6. What telemetry audit tools/methods do privacy users employ?
   - network traffic monitoring (mitmproxy, Wireshark, Little Snitch)
   - about:telemetry in Firefox
   - Community audits shared on forums

7. What was the 2025 Firefox Terms of Service controversy? What did Mozilla change, and what was the community response?

### Local-First / No-Cloud Demands

8. What sync solutions do privacy-focused users prefer?
   - Syncthing for bookmarks/history
   - Self-hosted Firefox Sync server
   - No sync at all
   - Encrypted cloud (Bitwarden-style)

9. What is the community stance on Firefox Sync, Chrome Sync, Brave Sync?

10. Do users demand local password management, local history search, local AI features (no cloud LLM calls)?

11. What local-first browser features are discussed as requirements in 2025-2026 privacy communities?
