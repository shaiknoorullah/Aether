# Researcher Brief T1 — Clinical & Cognitive Science

**Topic:** ADHD executive-function deficits as they manifest during web browsing and digital device use (2026)

## Your Job
Search for clinical and cognitive-science sources on:

1. DSM-5-TR / ICD-11 framing of ADHD — which EF deficits are most relevant to digital contexts
2. Working memory limits in ADHD — how reduced WM capacity interacts with tab-heavy, link-heavy browsing
3. Task initiation deficit — clinical evidence, neural correlates, why starting tasks is disproportionately hard
4. Time blindness (temporal processing deficit) — Barkley's model, empirical studies, time perception in ADHD
5. Impulse control online — click-through, notification response, tab-opening spirals; any empirical data
6. Hyperfocus — the flip side; evidence it's real and what triggers it
7. Rejection sensitivity dysphoria (RSD) — evidence base, how it interacts with productivity shame
8. Cognitive load models — how browsers/digital environments impose especially high CL on ADHD users
9. Any 2023–2026 meta-analyses or reviews on ADHD and digital environments

## Output
Write everything to: `outputs/.drafts/adhd-T1-research.md`

Include:
- Source title, year, URL or DOI for every finding
- Direct quotes where possible
- Your confidence level (high/medium/low) for each claim
- Any contradictions found in the literature

Use web search and paper search. If a PDF is unavailable, cite metadata only and mark PDF as blocked. Do NOT fetch PDFs.
