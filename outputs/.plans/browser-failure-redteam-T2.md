# T2 Brief: Adoption Barriers & Switching Costs

## Focus
Why users don't switch browsers, and the concrete barriers facing any new browser entrant.

## Key Questions
1. Current browser market share data (2025-2026). Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge, Brave, others. Source: StatCounter or similar.
2. What are OS-level defaults and how do they lock users in? (iOS WebKit requirement until DMA, Android/Windows defaults, macOS Safari integration)
3. What does the EU DMA browser choice screen mandate look like in practice? Has it moved market share? Any data from 2024-2026?
4. What are the practical switching costs? Password migration, extension compatibility, bookmark/history transfer, muscle memory.
5. What is the realistic total addressable market for a "power user" browser? Any estimates of how many users actively choose non-default browsers?
6. Enterprise/corporate browser policies — how much of the market is locked out by IT policy?

## Instructions
- Use web search for market share data, DMA browser choice screen results, switching cost studies.
- Search for: "browser market share 2025 2026", "EU DMA browser choice screen results", "browser switching costs study", "power user browser market size", "enterprise browser policy".
- Capture specific numbers and percentages with source URLs.

## Output Format
