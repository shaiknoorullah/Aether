# Researcher Brief T2 — HCI / UX Research

**Topic:** HCI and UX research on accessible browser/digital-interface design for ADHD and neurodivergent users

## Your Job
Search for HCI research, accessibility guidelines, and empirical UX studies on:

1. WCAG 2.x / COGA (Cognitive Accessibility) guidelines relevant to ADHD — specific provisions, design implications
2. Attention restoration theory (Kaplan & Kaplan) — relevance to browser design
3. Empirical HCI studies involving ADHD participants and digital interfaces (2018–2026)
4. Distraction reduction UI patterns — what has been studied empirically vs. only anecdotally
5. Focus mode / reader mode research — do they help ADHD users? Evidence?
6. Visual clutter and cognitive load studies in web contexts
7. Notification design — evidence on notification timing, batching, opt-in control for ADHD users
8. Browser history / memory aids — any research on externalized working memory tools for ND users
9. W3C COGA guidance documents (fetch the HTML page if accessible)
10. Any CHI, ASSETS, UIST, or CSCW papers on neurodivergent users and browser/web UX

## Output
Write everything to: `outputs/.drafts/adhd-T2-research.md`

Include:
- Source title, year, URL or DOI
- Direct quotes or paraphrased findings
- Confidence level per claim
- Note any contradictions or replication concerns

Use web search and paper search. Prefer HTML pages over PDFs. Do NOT fetch PDFs.
