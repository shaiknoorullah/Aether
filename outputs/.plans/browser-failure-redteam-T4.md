# T4 Brief: AI Browser Landscape, Regulatory Risk & Blind Spots

## Focus
The current AI-in-browsers landscape, regulatory factors, and implicit assumptions that could kill a project like Aether.

## Key Questions
1. What AI features have shipped in browsers as of 2025-2026? Chrome Gemini integration, Edge Copilot, Brave Leo, Arc Max, Opera Aria. What do users actually use vs. ignore?
2. Is there evidence of user demand for AI-native browsers, or is this supply-driven hype? Any survey data or usage metrics?
3. What AI features require local vs. cloud processing? Privacy implications of each.
4. Regulatory landscape: EU AI Act implications for browser AI features, GDPR constraints on AI data processing in browsers, US state privacy laws.
5. What are the implicit fatal assumptions for a project combining AI + privacy + power-user + new browser?
   - "Power users will pay for a browser"
   - "Privacy and AI can coexist without tradeoffs"
   - "There's a large enough market at the intersection of power-user AND privacy AND AI"
   - "A small team can maintain a browser fork"
   - "Users will trust a new entity with their browsing data for AI features"
   - "The AI capabilities will remain differentiated as incumbents add similar features"
6. Timing risk: where are we in the AI hype cycle for browser integration? Is this too early, too late, or right on time?
