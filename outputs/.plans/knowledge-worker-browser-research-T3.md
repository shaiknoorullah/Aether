# Researcher Brief T3 — Desired Browser Features: Surveys & User Research Rankings

## Your job
Find survey-backed or user-research-backed evidence on what browser features knowledge workers and researchers most want, are most frustrated by, and would most value.

1. **Browser frustration surveys**: What do users cite as biggest pain points? Search for surveys from browser vendors (Mozilla, Google, Microsoft), UX research firms, developer surveys (Stack Overflow), productivity tool companies.

2. **Desired features rankings**: AI summarization, tab grouping/management, vertical tabs, sidebar panels, built-in notes, better search/history, privacy controls, cross-device sync, reading mode, workspace/profile separation. Any ranked survey data from 2022–2026.

3. **Power user vs casual user**: Do knowledge workers/researchers have meaningfully different browser preferences than casual users? Any segmentation data.

4. **Browser switching reasons**: Why do people switch browsers? What does that reveal about unmet needs? Mozilla/Google/StatCounter/Statista data.

5. **AI feature adoption in browsers**: Have users adopted AI summarization, AI search sidebars, AI writing assistants in browsers? Microsoft Copilot in Edge adoption data, Brave Leo usage, Opera Aria usage. What do they use vs. what do they ignore?

6. **Productivity and focus features**: Do Not Disturb modes, focus modes, distraction blocking — survey adoption and satisfaction data.
