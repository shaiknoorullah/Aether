# Researcher Brief T1: Economic Burden, Productivity Loss & Presenteeism — Adult ADHD (2020–2026)

## Your job
Search for peer-reviewed and high-quality grey-literature evidence on the **economic burden** of adult ADHD and its impact on **workplace productivity**, including presenteeism and absenteeism. Focus on 2020–2026 but include landmark pre-2020 studies as context.

## Key questions to answer
1. What are the estimated annual societal/economic costs of adult ADHD (US, EU, global)? Which cost-of-illness studies have been published 2020–2026?
2. What is the magnitude of productivity loss (presenteeism, absenteeism) attributable to adult ADHD vs controls? Report effect sizes, WPAI scores, or comparable metrics.
3. How do healthcare utilization and direct costs compare for adults with ADHD vs matched controls?
4. What is the economic impact on employers (HR costs, turnover, disability claims)?
5. Any meta-analyses or systematic reviews of economic burden, 2020–2026?

## Search strategy
Use paper search (academic) AND web search. Try queries including:
- "adult ADHD economic burden 2020 2021 2022 2023 2024"
- "ADHD presenteeism absenteeism workplace productivity"
- "ADHD cost of illness societal cost"
- "ADHD WPAI work productivity activity impairment"
- "ADHD employer cost turnover"
- Search alphaXiv/arXiv and web for recent systematic reviews

## Output format
Write a structured research note to: `outputs/.drafts/adhd-productivity-digital-interventions-research-T1.md`

Include:
- For each finding: claim, source title, authors, year, journal/venue, URL or DOI
- Confidence level (HIGH = RCT/meta-analysis, MEDIUM = cohort/survey, LOW = grey lit/editorial)
- Direct quotes of key numbers where available
- Any methodological caveats
- List of all search queries used
- Mark any URL as UNVERIFIED if you could not confirm it loaded

## Critical rules
- Do NOT parse or fetch PDF URLs directly — cite the abstract page or DOI landing page instead
- Do NOT invent numbers, citations, or studies
- If a paper cannot be found, record it as NOT FOUND rather than fabricating details
- Prefer HTML abstract pages (PubMed, PsycINFO, Cochrane, journal sites) over PDFs
- Keep every claimed number traceable to a URL
