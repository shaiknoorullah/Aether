# Researcher Brief T4 — Shame vs. Support Design Philosophy

**Topic:** How shame vs. support framing in productivity/focus software affects neurodivergent users; neurodiversity-affirming design principles

## Your Job
Research the following:

1. Shame and productivity software — evidence that punitive/gamified approaches harm ADHD users (e.g., streaks, failure alerts, "you failed your goal" messaging)
2. Self-determination theory (Deci & Ryan) applied to ADHD — autonomy, competence, relatedness and how software design supports or undermines these
3. Neurodiversity-affirming design — what does this mean in software? Key advocates, any published frameworks
4. Motivational design for ADHD: what reward structures, feedback cadences, and framing have evidence of effectiveness?
5. Stigma and ADHD — research on internalized stigma, how UI language (e.g., "productivity score") affects self-perception
6. Positive vs. negative reinforcement in habit-tracking apps for ADHD users — any studies
7. "Scaffolding" as a design metaphor — how software can externalize EF without shaming
8. Community voice: what do ADHD adults and advocates say they want from software (ADDitude, CHADD, Understood.org, Reddit ADHD communities)
9. IDEO / design-for-disability literature on co-design with neurodivergent users
10. Any 2023–2026 publications on ADHD and digital wellbeing, smartphone use, or app design ethics

## Output
Write everything to: `outputs/.drafts/adhd-T4-research.md`

Include:
- Source title, year, URL or DOI
- Direct quotes where possible
- Confidence level per claim
- Note any advocacy-vs-evidence tensions

Use web search and paper search. Prefer HTML pages. Do NOT fetch PDFs.
