## Verification Results

### Claim 1: Context switching costs 40% of productive time (CMU/UC Irvine)
- **Original Source**: Discovery / Market Data reports
- **Verification Questions**: What is the origin of the 40% figure? Is it from CMU or UC Irvine?
- **Independent Evidence**: The 40% figure originates from **David Meyer (University of Michigan)** via the American Psychological Association, not CMU or UC Irvine. APA states: "even brief mental blocks created by shifting between tasks can cost as much as 40 percent of someone's productive time." This is widely cited across productivity research.
- **Verdict**: PARTIALLY_VERIFIED
- **Confidence**: 75%
- **Notes**: The 40% figure is real and well-sourced (APA/Meyer), but the attribution to "CMU/UC Irvine" is incorrect. CMU's tab study is separate; UC Irvine (Gloria Mark) is associated with the 23-minute refocus finding, not the 40% figure. The statistic also represents a theoretical upper bound ("as much as 40%"), not an average.

### Claim 2: 23 minutes 15 seconds to refocus after interruption (Gloria Mark)
- **Original Source**: Discovery reports
- **Verification Questions**: Did Gloria Mark publish this finding? What was the study methodology?
- **Independent Evidence**: Confirmed. Gloria Mark (UC Irvine) found that interrupted work is resumed on average in 23 minutes and 15 seconds. Study observed 36 knowledge workers across multiple days. Published findings cited by Gallup and Fast Company with consistent figures. The 2008 follow-up paper (Mark, Gudith, Klocke) further validated the interruption cost model.
- **Verdict**: VERIFIED
- **Confidence**: 95%
- **Notes**: Widely replicated citation. The 82% same-day resumption rate is also confirmed.

### Claim 3: 25% of users experienced browser crashes from too many tabs (CMU study)

- **Independent Evidence**: Confirmed. Study involved 103 participants in surveys and interviews. Published on cmu.edu.
- **Verdict**: VERIFIED
- **Confidence**: 95%
- **Notes**: Exact match. Source: https://www.cmu.edu/news/stories/archives/2021/may/overcoming-tab-overload.html

### Claim 4: 81% of consumers willing to switch browsers (Shift 2026)

### Claim 6: Firefox declined from 32% (2009) to 2.25% (2025)

### Claim 10: 62% digital burnout rate tied to browser/notification overload
- **Original Source**: Market Data reports

- **Independent Evidence**: Shift's 2026 State of Browsing Report found "62% of users experience digital burnout either occasionally or regularly." ### Claim 11: 1,200 app switches per day for average digital worker (HBR)

### Claim 12: Browser agent 10-step workflow success rate is ~19.7%
- **Original Source**: Discovery / Technical reports
- **Verification Questions**: Is this a benchmark result or theoretical calculation?
- **Independent Evidence**: The 19.7% is a **mathematical derivation** (0.85^10), not an empirical benchmark result. It comes from a Checksum/CIOInfluence analysis (Dec 2025): "At 85% accuracy per action, end-to-end success is 0.85^10 = 19.7%." Actual WebArena benchmarks show different figures: original GPT-4 baseline was 14.41%, current SOTA is ~60-72%.
- **Verdict**: PARTIALLY_VERIFIED
- **Confidence**: 65%
- **Notes**: The math is correct but the framing as a "success rate" is misleading. It's a compounding-error illustration assuming 85% per-step accuracy, not an observed benchmark. Real browser agents achieve different rates depending on the system.

### Claim 13: Token consumption: MCP 114K tokens vs CLI 27K tokens per task
- **Original Source**: Technical reports
- **Verification Questions**: What is the source of these specific token counts?
- **Independent Evidence**: Confirmed via Microsoft's Playwright benchmarks, reported by TestDino: "a typical browser automation task consumed roughly 114,000 tokens with MCP versus about 27,000 tokens with CLI." Represents ~4x reduction. Root cause: MCP injects full tool catalog into context per request.
- **Verdict**: VERIFIED
- **Confidence**: 85%
- **Notes**: Specific to Playwright MCP vs Playwright CLI for browser automation. Not generalizable to all MCP vs CLI comparisons. The gap narrows with newer MCP optimization techniques (Anthropic's tool-search approach reduces to 2K tokens).

The general uniqueness rate was 84%, and later studies with better sampling found much lower rates (~33%). Citing 94% as current "fingerprinting accuracy" is misleading in a 2026 context.

## Contradictions Found

No claims were outright contradicted, but several contain significant caveats:

1. **Claim 1** (40% context switching): Correct figure, wrong attribution (Meyer/APA, not CMU/UC Irvine).
2. **Claim 6** (Firefox decline): Mixes all-platform 2025 figure with desktop-only 2009 figure — methodologically inconsistent comparison.
3. **Claim 23** (94% fingerprinting): Applies to obsolete browser configurations (Flash/Java enabled). Later studies with better methodology found ~33% uniqueness.
4. **Claim 25** (275 interruptions): Applies to top 20% most-interrupted workers, not the average knowledge worker as commonly cited.
